# Resume Reference Example
## Skill 02: Resume Writer | Structural and Style Model

This is the canonical example of a correctly written resume produced by this skill. Use it as the structural and stylistic model for every resume you generate. Names, employers, and accomplishments are illustrative: the format, tone, writing discipline, and section logic are the standard.

---

## What This Example Demonstrates

- Professional summary that leads with identity, not adjectives
- Core competencies formatted as functional groupings, not skill dumps
- Signature projects section with named deliverables, one-line descriptions, and quantified bullets
- Experience bullets that lead with action verbs and close with scale or outcome
- No em dashes anywhere in the document
- No filler language: no "passionate," "driven," "results-oriented," "synergy," "leverage" as a verb
- Certifications and additional section that includes differentiators (veteran status, founder role)
- Clean, scannable structure that works for both human readers and ATS systems

## Annotation Notes for Skill 02

**Summary:** Leads with functional identity ("Enterprise operations leader") and years of experience. States the type of work and the scale of output. Closes with two differentiators. No adjectives. No claims that are not immediately supported by the specifics that follow.

**Core Competencies:** Three rows, grouped by function. Each entry is Category: Item • Item • Item. Not a flat list of tools. Not a keyword dump. Grouped so a reader understands what the candidate does, not just what software they have touched.

**Signature Projects:** Each project has a name (noun phrase describing the deliverable), a one-line description (what it replaced and what it delivers), and two to three bullets (outcomes, not activities). Every bullet has a number. If the candidate cannot provide a number for a bullet, that bullet is rewritten as an activity bullet in the experience section instead: it does not belong in Signature Projects without a quantified outcome.

**Experience bullets:** Every bullet leads with a past-tense action verb. Scale or outcome is in the same clause as the action. No bullet begins with "Responsible for," "Helped," "Worked with," or "Was instrumental in." No em dashes. Commas and periods only.

**Certifications and Additional:** This section captures credentials, a founder role if applicable, and veteran status. It is not an education overflow section. Keep it short: three to five lines maximum.

**Education:** Degree, field, institution, year. One line. No GPA unless the candidate is a recent graduate and the GPA is above 3.5.

**What is not in this resume:**
- An "Objective" statement (replaced by the Professional Summary)
- "References available upon request" (assumed and omits space)
- A photo
- A table of contents
- Any section titled "Skills" as a flat list
- Any use of "I" anywhere in the document
